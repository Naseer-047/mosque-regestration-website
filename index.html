Rules & Regulation

Eligibility Criteria

  • The mosque must be located within the boundaries of the concerned Taluka.
  • The applicant must be a resident of the same Taluka or authorized by the Mosque Committee.
  • The mosque must be intended for public religious use and not for personal or private property use.
  • The land or building must be legally owned, leased, or donated for religious purposes.
  • The mosque should not cause public inconvenience, encroach on government property, or block public roads.

Documents Required

  • Ownership or Lease Certificate of the property.
  • NOC (No Objection Certificate) from local residents or Panchayat/Municipality.
  • ID Proof of the applicant or managing trustee (Aadhaar, Voter ID, etc.).
  • Blueprint / Layout Plan of the mosque building and premises.
  • Electricity Bill / Water Bill / Address Proof of the site.
  • List of Committee Members with their contact details and designations.
  • Declaration of Compliance with Waqf Board or local religious authority (if applicable).

Registration Procedure

  • The applicant must fill out the registration form online or at the Taluka Religious Affairs Office.
  • All required documents must be attached and verified by the local verification officer.
  • After verification, the application will be forwarded to the Taluka Officer or Waqf Committee for approval.
  • Once approved, a Registration Number and Certificate will be issued to the mosque.
  • The mosque committee must display the registration certificate in a visible place within the mosque.

Responsibilities of the Mosque Committee

  • Maintain daily records of prayers, events, and donations transparently.
  • Ensure cleanliness, discipline, and respect within the mosque premises.
  • Conduct religious gatherings peacefully without disturbing nearby residents.
  • Keep emergency contacts and safety measures (fire extinguisher, first aid) available.
  • Inform the Taluka Office about any change in committee members or building modification within 30 days.

Prohibited Activities

  • No political, commercial, or non-religious gatherings are allowed in mosque premises.
  • No misuse of mosque property for illegal, hate, or divisive activities.
  • The mosque shall not collect donations without proper receipt or committee approval..
  • Any violation of government or Waqf laws may lead to cancellation of registration.

Renewal & Updates

  • Registration should be renewed every 3 years or as per Taluka guidelines.
  • Updated documents and committee details must be submitted during renewal.
  • Failure to renew may result in temporary suspension of recognition.

Grievance and Appeal

  • If an application is rejected, the applicant may appeal within 30 days to the Taluka Religious Officer or District Authority.
  • Complaints or disputes should be addressed in writing to the Taluka Mosque Registration Committee.
